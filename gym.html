Workout Plans

I follow the **PUSH, LEG, PULL** split to target each muscle group twice a week. This split helps divide the weekly volume and allows for proper muscle recovery. According to research, muscle recovery typically takes **2 days**, so this plan ensures that each muscle is trained effectively without overtraining. 

PUSH:
INCLINED BENCH PRESS (3x8)
MACHINE CHEST PRESS (3x8)
CABLE FLYS (3x12)
TRICEPS EXTENSIONS (3x10)
CABLE LATERAL RAISES (5x12)
TRICEPS PUSHDOWN (3x10)

PULL:
LAT PULL DOWNS (5x10)
SEATED ROWS (4x10)
LAT PULLOVERS (3x10)
DUMBBELL ROWS (3x10) / DEADLIFTS (4x8)
PREACHER CURLS (4x10)
INCLINED DUMBBELL CURLS (3x8)
FACEPULLS (3x10)

LEG:
HAMSTRING CURLS (5x10)
SQUATS (5x10) / LEG PRESS (5x10)
LEG EXTENSIONS (3x15)
CALF RAISES (3x10)
OVH SHOULDER PRESS (4x10)
LUNGES (3x10)

Nutrition Tips

To fuel your gains, it's essential to focus on your nutrition. Here are some of the tips I follow:

  • Complete your daily protein goals with sources like curd, kidney beans, oats, soya chunks, whey protein, and milk.
  • Maintain a slight calorie deficit of **100-200 calories** below maintenance for fat loss.
  • Incorporate daily cardio to help with overall fitness and fat loss.
  • Drink **4-5 liters** of water per day to stay hydrated.

Workout Tips

Here are some strategies that have worked for me:

  • Stay focused during your workouts—avoid unnecessary conversations to save time.
  • Take each set to failure for maximum intensity and muscle engagement.
  • Once you achieve 8 reps on a compound exercise, increase the weight by **2.5 kg** (progressive overload).
  • Track each workout in a notepad app to monitor your progress.
  • Stay consistent! Your body won’t change overnight, but with dedication and perseverance, the results will come.
